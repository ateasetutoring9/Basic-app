## What is Algebra?

Algebra is the branch of mathematics that uses **letters** (called variables) to stand in for unknown numbers. Instead of writing "some number plus 3 equals 7", we write a much shorter equation: x + 3 = 7. The letter x represents the number we are trying to find.

This might sound abstract, but you already use algebraic thinking in everyday life. If you know a bag of apples costs $2 more than a bag of oranges, and together they cost $10, you are automatically solving an algebra problem in your head.

---

## Key Vocabulary

Before solving equations, it helps to know the names of the parts:

| Term | What it means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Variable** | A letter that represents an unknown number | x, y, n, t |
| **Expression** | A combination of numbers and variables (no equals sign) | 3x + 5 |
| **Equation** | Two expressions connected by an equals sign | 3x + 5 = 14 |
| **Coefficient** | The number multiplied by a variable | In 3x, the coefficient is 3 |
| **Constant** | A plain number with no variable | In 3x + 5, the constant is 5 |
| **Solution** | The value of the variable that makes the equation true | x = 3 |

---

## The Golden Rule of Algebra

> **Whatever you do to one side of an equation, you must do the same to the other side.**

Think of an equation as a set of scales. As long as you add, subtract, multiply, or divide both pans equally, the scales stay balanced.

---

## Worked Example 1 — One Step (Addition)

**Problem:** Solve x + 8 = 15

**Step 1:** We want x on its own. It currently has 8 added to it, so we subtract 8 from both sides.

x + 8 - 8 = 15 - 8

**Step 2:** Simplify.

x = 7

**Check:** Substitute back into the original: 7 + 8 = 15. True, so x = 7 is correct.

---

## Worked Example 2 — One Step (Multiplication)

**Problem:** Solve 4n = 28

**Step 1:** n is being multiplied by 4. To undo multiplication, divide both sides by 4.

4n / 4 = 28 / 4

**Step 2:** Simplify.

n = 7

**Check:** 4 × 7 = 28. Correct.

---

## Worked Example 3 — Two Steps

**Problem:** Solve 2x + 3 = 11

With two-step equations, work backwards through the order of operations:
1. First undo addition or subtraction.
2. Then undo multiplication or division.

**Step 1:** Subtract 3 from both sides (undo the + 3).

2x + 3 - 3 = 11 - 3
2x = 8

**Step 2:** Divide both sides by 2 (undo the × 2).

2x / 2 = 8 / 2
x = 4

**Check:** 2(4) + 3 = 8 + 3 = 11. Correct.

## Writing Your Own Expressions

You can turn word problems into algebra by picking a variable for the unknown:

- "A number increased by 6" → n + 6
- "Three times a number" → 3n
- "Five less than twice a number" → 2n - 5

Once you have the expression, set it equal to what the problem tells you the answer is, and solve.

---

## Summary

- Variables are letters that hold the place of unknown numbers.
- Expressions combine numbers and variables; equations have an equals sign.
- To solve an equation, isolate the variable by applying inverse operations equally to both sides.
- Always check your answer by substituting it back into the original equation.
